Methodology

How The Forecast Works

The site combines historical international results, team-strength features, recent form, venue context, weather, and live football feeds where available. The public forecast is an ensemble of a win/draw/loss model and a score-grid model.

Win / draw / lossEstimates match outcome probabilities from team strength and context.
Score forecastUses a Poisson goals model to project expected goals and likely scorelines.
Similarity checkCompares fixtures with similar historical matches as a challenger model.

This is a forecasting and research project. It does not guarantee accuracy, and it should not be treated as financial advice.

Reading The Forecast

  • Win probability is the model-estimated chance that a specific team wins the match.
  • Draw probability is the model-estimated chance that the match ends level.
  • Prediction strength is the top outcome probability grouped into plain language. It is not a guarantee.
  • Model agreement counts how many baseline models point to the same winner or draw.
  • Expected goals is the model average score, not the single most likely score.
  • Most likely score is the exact scoreline with the largest probability in the score grid.
  • Over 2.5 goals means the match has at least 3 total goals.
  • Both teams to score means each team scores at least one goal.

Forecasts are probabilistic and should be used for research, discussion, and model review. They are not financial advice or guaranteed betting edges.
