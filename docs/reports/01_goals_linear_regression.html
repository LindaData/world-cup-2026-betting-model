Team goals scored from strength, opponent strength, and match context

1 Purpose

This report documents the goals model for the World Cup prediction project.

The target is team goals scored in a historical international match:

\[ Y_{i,m} = \text{goals scored by team } i \text{ in match } m \]

Goals are non-negative counts. The distribution report compares count-model shapes used for soccer scoring.

2 Unit Of Observation

The modeling frame has one row per team-match.

If Argentina plays France, the data has two rows:

  • Argentina’s goals as the target, with France as the opponent.
  • France’s goals as the target, with Argentina as the opponent.

4 Elo Feature

For one team against one opponent, the expected result before kickoff is:

\[ p = \frac{1}{1 + 10^{-\left(\text{Team Elo} + \text{Home Adv.} - \text{Opponent Elo}\right)/400}} \]

Where:

  • Team Elo is the team’s pre-match rating.
  • Opponent Elo is the opponent’s pre-match rating.
  • Home Adv. is the home advantage adjustment.
  • p is the expected result on a 0 to 1 scale.

5 Model

The regression is:

\[ \text{Goals} = \beta_0 + \beta_1\text{Team Elo} + \beta_2\text{Opponent Elo} + \beta_3\text{Expected Result} + \beta_4\text{Listed Home} + \beta_5\text{Neutral Site} + \epsilon \]

with:

\[ \epsilon_{i,m} \sim N(0, \sigma^2) \]

7 Interpretation

The signs match soccer intuition:

  • opponent_pre_elo is negative: stronger opponents suppress scoring.
  • pre_match_expected_result is positive: teams expected to perform better score more.
  • listed_homeTRUE is positive: listed home teams score more.
